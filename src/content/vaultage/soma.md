## thoughts

#### "this album is kinda..."
when i first heard this album (the second it came out in my region btw) i was... a little disappointed. before listening to it, it was available in
other parts of the world before me (for example nz) and it was also on the bassland telegram which i didnt know about at the time, and the skrillcord
discord server was talking about how underwhelming it was and how everything was all over the place, and how some songs didnt fit at all. and ALOT of people
were flaming the album cover for looking off and... not exactly like a skrillex album cover. have a look at it. it looks *really* edgy and random. like,
i can see why people have this opinion.

i honestly couldnt believe people were flaming it before i listened to it because, it couldnt be THAT bad right??

<br>
right...?
<br>
<br>
  
so after my 1st listen of the album, i actually understood the criticism from everyone, and lowkey agreed with it. 
yes there were some absolute BANGERS but it felt like it was thrown together at the last minute.
i gave the album another listen and was still a little dissapointed, then i went to bed because it was 2am.

#### "actually nvm this is super good"

throughout that day i listened to it about 7 times total. and i can tell you, my opinion DRASTICALLY changed from what it was before.

im writing this on 7/6/26 (2 days after release) at 1:30am and i can tell you this is genuinely a great album. yes its different. yes its
very experimental. yes its not like what skrillex usually sounds like. but atp he's just releasing because he can and he's free from his
15 year contract with atlantic records. in fact, after this album dropped he sent a dropbox link to everyone on his email list that contained
a *.zip of the .wav files for each song*. its actually insane this man is just giving us music for free.

so yeah anyways, back to my thoughts on the actual album. yeah, its not perfect but it works. its not meant to be the best thing in the world.
its basically meant to be skrillex trying stuff out and experimenting. there were some interesting choices that were made in terms of song order,
album cover, and... vocalists. but im 99% sure thats the point. normally skrillex and other adjacent producers put emotional/happy ending stuff
at the end of the album—but this time he did something different. yeah, Duro is pretty happy ending type, and its the second last track.
the last song is actually (what most people have been calling it so far) a modern version of Doompy Poomp. in other words, a troll song.
that kinda pissed people off because the vocals (Naisa and BEAM) sound so out of place and silly and annoying. i think its a fun little track. not something
i would play in front of anyone to introduce them to skrillex but i guess its a little blooper/bonus track type of thing (so maybe Duro was the last song in
a sense.)

and also, people hating on Tranki? yeah nah that song is actually great. Vocals are questionable and sound off but thats literally what the track is trying to do. plus the second half of the song is actually brilliant. not the best song on the album but still super duper good.
i kinda hated it when i listened for the 1st time but i guess it just grew on me.

on top of that, alot of people were saying Scut 2 feels out of place. yeah, im gonna agree on that. coming right in the middle of Noche Without You and
Cheeni, its a little weird to put there, considering those two songs are the sorta happy ending/emotional type i was talking about earlier.
people were (yet again) complaining that it wasnt the original Scut ID, but a literal bootleg from this guy called "rom". honestly?
i really dont give a fuck. just be grateful he's actually putting out music bro. the track is riddim that tickles my brain, and it sounds off and wonky but ITS SO SO GOOD??? yeah i dont really like where it was put on the tracklist (again, probably intentional... but whatever), but as for the song *itself*, yeah nah this absolutely slaps.


oh does it stop? people were talking about how La Noche 2 "adds nothing to the album" dude actually shut the fuck up, its a beautiful track and a buildup
to Anybody. this was the same La Noche 2 skrillex emailed out to his list a while ago. just shut up and enjoy the song dude

Pente Rala was kinda in the middle—no one was hating on it but no one was really talking about it either. yeah, its a good track. i like the flutter bass
and the catchy prechorus lyrics. its approved

É o Bonde was literally La Noche 1.5. just listen to it. very similar bass notes and groove. people were saying it was a generic house track
but i think it slaps honestly. the saws do a good job at adding filth to the song. also shoutout RHR bro has been cooking lately
